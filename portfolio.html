About

Quantum chemistry meets generative AI

I'm an undergraduate at SPIT and an IRCC Fellow at IIT Bombay, working on problems that sit at the intersection of quantum chemistry algorithms and modern machine learning.

My core research focuses on Sample-Based Diagonalization (SBD) — improving and extending the DICE/SHCI solver, developing basis-set transfer pipelines for CI wavefunctions, and building Transformer-based generative models that learn the mapping from small-basis to large-basis dominant determinants.

I have a long-term bet on Scientific Machine Learning as the foundational framework for the next generation of quantum simulation tools.

Research & Projects

R-01
Basis-Set Transfer for SHCI Wavefunctions

Developing a pipeline to transfer converged DICE/SHCI wavefunctions from a smaller basis (6-31G) to a larger basis (cc-pVDZ), intelligently seeding the variational space using HCI heat-bath selection criteria and geometric cutoff schedules — avoiding combinatorial explosion in determinant enumeration.

DICE SHCI Basis Set Transfer Variational Monte Carlo
R-02
Transformer-Based Determinant Generator

A generative ML model treating determinants as sequences of orbital occupation tokens, with an autoregressive decoder trained on cc-pVDZ DICE solutions. Orbital energy serves as positional encoding. The goal: learn the small-basis → large-basis dominant determinant mapping, bypassing the Hamiltonian selection bottleneck.

Transformer SciML Generative Model Quantum Chemistry
R-03
PG-LSCI: Supervised Importance Scoring for CI Expansion

A supervised MLP scoring determinants by PT2 signal for targeted CI space expansion. Phase A demonstrates ROC-AUC ~0.998 and near-perfect Recall@K on held-out geometries. Phases B and C extend to conditional generative models and cross-basis transferability.

MLP PT2 CI Expansion Transfer Learning
R-04
FMO2 Fragment Molecular Orbital Parallelization

Parallelizing the FMO2 workflow around the architectural constraint that DICE calls MPI internally — resolved via subprocess.Popen with isolated working directories, staggered launches, and per-job namespace isolation to avoid MPI bootstrap race conditions.

FMO2 MPI HPC Python
R-05
EEG / P300 Statistical Analysis Pipeline

Building a P300 statistical analysis pipeline across Korean, Giga, and AdaptiveP300 datasets on AWS S3, using Linear Mixed Effects models with careful decisions around bin treatment, regional averaging, and FDR correction.

EEG BCI LME Models EEGLAB AWS
